Aluminum vs. Steel vs. Wood — What’s the Best Material?

When choosing a pergola, patio cover, or carport, the material determines durability, maintenance, lifespan, and long-term cost.
Here’s why Skyaluxe uses architectural-grade aluminum for all structural components.

  • ✔
    Durability & Lifespan
  • ✔
    Maintenance Requirements
  • ✔
    Fire, Water & Mold Safety
  • ✔
    Structural Performance
  • ✔
    Weather Resistance
  • ✔
    Total Cost of Ownership

Durability & Lifespan

Material Lifespan Issues
Steel 15-25 years Rusts, corrodes, heavy, requires coatings
Wood 10-15 years Rot, mold, warping, insects, cracking
Aluminum 40-50+ years Rust-free, weatherproof, impact-resistant

Maintenance Requirements

Material Lifespan
Steel Require repainting, rust removal, powder-coating
Wood Requires staining, sealing, painting every 1–2 years
Aluminum Zero maintenance — just wash occasionally

Weather Resistance

Material Rain Snow Sun Humidity
Steel Rust risk Strong but heavy Expands/contracts in heat Rusts in humidity
Wood Absorbs water Warps under snow load Fades in sun Mold + insects
Aluminum Waterproof Engineered snow load UV-resistant Doesn’t absorb moisture

Structural Performance

Material Weight Strength Flexibility
Steel Very heavy High Too rigid (cracks welds)
Wood Light but weak Moderate Moderate
Aluminum Lightweight High Controlled

Fire, Water & Mold Safety

Material Risks
Steel Rusts, heats up, needs coatings
Wood Burns, molds, rots, attracts termites
Aluminum Non-combustible, rust-free, mold-free

Total Cost of Ownership

Material Initial Cost Maintenance Repair Lifespan Cost
Steel Medium–High High Medium High
Wood Low Very High High Highest
Aluminum Medium $0 Very Low Lowest

Conclusion

Aluminum is the best long-term investment for any outdoor structure. It delivers: Zero maintenance, 50+ year durability, Modern architectural look, No rust, no rot, no warping, Superior performance in all U.S. climates, Better resale value for your home
